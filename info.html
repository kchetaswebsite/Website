Monuments Info

Here we provided outlined informaion of each monument.



Charminar

Charminar also known as four minars in urdu was built in 1591 by Muḥammad Qulī Quṭb Shah, the fifth king of the Quṭb Shāhī dynasty, reported as the first building in Hyderabad, his new capital. It is considerd one of the greatest architectural achievements of the Qutub shahi era. Today it is located in the old city, where there Bazars around the monument.

Qutub shahi tombs

The Qutub shahi tombs were plaanned and built by the monarchs of The Qutubshahi dynasty, who has ruled Hyderabad nearly 170 years.The first Tomb was built by The founder of the dynasty, Mohammad Quli Qutub Shah who had planned his own tomb. There are 7 tombs dedicated to the 7 rulers of the The Qutubshahi dynasty and their famalies.

Mecca Masjid

One of the oldest mosques in the city, the Mecca Masjid in Hyderabad is one of the largest Masjids in India. This revered shrine was commissioned by Muhammad Quli Qutub Shah, the fifth ruler of the Qutub Shahi Dynasty in the year 1614, while the construction saw completion in the year 1694.

Falaknuma palace

Falaknuma palace, now a 7 star hotel before it was the palace of The Nizam rulers, it was built in 3 March 1884. Before the nizams had the palace it was owned by Sir Viqar ul-Umar, the prime minister of the princly state of hyderabad. The architect of the palace was William Ward Marrer.

Chowmahalla palace

The Chowmahalla palace was nutily between 1857 and 1869 by the Nizams, the grandeur of the Chowmahalla Palace is a living witness to the luxury in the Nizam dynasty. The luxurious palace was once the seat of the Asaf Jahi dynasty till its fall.

Hussain Sagar

The lake Hussain Sagar connects Hyderabad to Secunderabad. It was built during the reign of Ibrahim Quli Qutub Shah by Hussain Shah Wali in 1562 AD. It is situated in the heart of hyderabad it is one of the largest man made lakes in India.

Warangal Fort

The Warangal Fort was built by the Kakatiya King Ganapati Deva during the 13th century. The history of Warangal fort is symbolic of the history of the Kakatiya rule. The fort had resisted multiple attacks by the delhi sultanate. It was the capitol till the Quli Qutub shahi dyansty had moved to Golconda Fort.

Golconda fort

Golconda fort was bulit in 13th century by the Kakatiya dynasty. The word Golla Conda translates to Shepherd's Hill as it was called by the locals in telugu. The kakatiya's may have built Golconda fort but it became now what it is thanks to Mohammad Quli Qutub Shah, who made Golconda his capital.
